Computational structural biologist integrating multiscale molecular simulations, bioinformatics, and artificial intelligence to understand and design bioactive peptides and protein systems.

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher and Lab Manager at Kansas State University, working at the interface of computational structural biology, bioinformatics, peptide science, and artificial intelligence. My research focuses on understanding how proteins and peptides behave in complex biological environments and using this knowledge to design functional biomolecules for therapeutic and applied systems.

My work combines all-atom and coarse-grained molecular simulations, bioinformatics, protein language models, deep learning, and graph-based approaches to connect molecular mechanisms with predictive modeling. I study protein–membrane interactions, peptide-induced pore formation, protein aggregation, and molecular recognition at complex interfaces, including heterogeneous solvent, membrane, pressure, and ice–water environments.

Current research directions

  1. AI-driven peptide prediction and design – developing pLM4CPPs and pLM4G-CPPs by integrating protein language models with graph learning for accurate prediction and design of cell-penetrating and antimicrobial peptides.
  2. Multiscale simulations of proteins, membranes, and complex environments – using all-atom and coarse-grained modeling to study gluten proteins, ovalbumin, heterogeneous lipid bilayers, antifreeze proteins, and membrane-active peptides under solvent, pressure, processing, and interfacial conditions.
  3. Open-source platforms for drug discovery and non-covalent interactions – contributing to the Molecular Property Diagnostic Suite (MPDS), A2ID, CAD, and fragment libraries to explore chemical space and quantify key non-covalent interactions in protein–ligand systems.

Research vision: My research aims to develop integrative multiscale and AI-driven frameworks that connect sequence, structure, dynamics, and function in proteins and peptides. By combining physics-based simulations, bioinformatics, protein language models, and graph learning, I aim to enable predictive and generative design of bioactive molecules for applications in therapeutics, biomolecular engineering, and food systems.

I obtained my Ph.D. in Computational Biology from CSIR-Indian Institute of Chemical Technology under the supervision of Prof. G. Narahari Sastry. My doctoral research focused on ion–molecule interactions, lipid membrane heterogeneity, and computational approaches for drug repurposing in infectious diseases. Prior to this, I earned an M.Sc. in Bioinformatics (Gold Medalist) from Central University of Bihar and a B.Sc. in Biotechnology from Magadh University. I have also contributed to the development of computational platforms and databases supporting drug discovery and non-covalent interaction analysis at CSIR-NEIST.

Research expertise

  • Computational structural biology & biomolecular dynamics – all-atom and coarse-grained MD of proteins, peptides, and membranes under environmental stresses (pressure, solvent, processing) to link conformational dynamics with function, stability, and allergenicity.
  • Protein & peptide structure–function prediction – multi-scale modeling of protein–protein, protein–ligand, and protein–membrane interactions, including QM/MM and enhanced-sampling approaches for binding and selectivity.
  • AI-driven bioinformatics & peptide design – development of predictive models using protein language models, graph neural networks, and classical ML for cell-penetrating, antimicrobial, and bioactive peptides.
  • Computational drug discovery & cheminformatics – virtual screening, drug repurposing, and fragment-based design; exploration of chemical space using MPDS, RDKit, and related tools for small-molecule optimization.
  • Membrane biophysics & biologics design – modeling lipid heterogeneity, peptide–bilayer interactions, and pore formation to guide the design of targeted biologics and membrane-active agents.
  • Software, databases & open platforms – co-development of web portals and databases (MPDS, A2ID, CAD) for non-covalent interactions and structure-based drug discovery, supporting academic and industrial users.

Research highlights

  • pLM4CPPs & pLM4G-CPPs. Developed protein language model–based CNN and graph models for cell-penetrating peptide prediction, improving over existing CPP predictors and enabling interpretable sequence–feature analyses.
  • Membrane selectivity of antimicrobial peptides. Used AA/CG MD to uncover how Snakin-Z and melittin discriminate between prokaryotic and eukaryotic membranes, integrating contact analysis, pore formation, and pressure-dependent effects.
  • Gliadin and protein dynamics in complex environments. Performed long-timescale simulations of α-gliadin and ovalbumin under ethanol, high pressure, and ultrasound, linking structural changes to functionality and potential allergenicity.
  • MPDS & non-covalent interaction databases. Contributed to the Molecular Property Diagnostic Suite, A2ID, CAD, and fragment libraries for systematic exploration of chemical space and non-covalent interactions.
