Computational structural biologist developing integrative multiscale modeling, bioinformatics, and AI-driven frameworks to decode, predict, and design functional proteins and peptides across complex biological systems.

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher and Lab Manager at Kansas State University, where I develop computational approaches to understand how proteins and peptides function in complex biological environments and how these mechanisms can be translated into the rational design of therapeutic, bioactive, and applied biomolecular systems. My work combines multiscale molecular simulations, structural bioinformatics, protein language models, machine learning, and graph-based modeling to connect molecular mechanisms with predictive and generative models across diverse biomolecular systems.

Current research directions

  1. AI-driven peptide prediction and design – building protein language model- and graph-based frameworks for predicting and engineering cell-penetrating, antimicrobial, and bioactive peptides.
  2. Multiscale biomolecular modeling – using all-atom and coarse-grained simulations to investigate protein–membrane interactions, peptide-induced pore formation, protein aggregation, and interfacial recognition.
  3. Computational platforms and interaction analysis – contributing to databases and tools for non-covalent interactions, chemical-space exploration, drug repurposing, and structure-based discovery.

Research vision: I aim to establish a research program that bridges molecular simulation, bioinformatics, and artificial intelligence to connect sequence, structure, dynamics, and function, enabling predictive and generative design of bioactive molecules for therapeutics, biomolecular engineering, and food systems.

I obtained my Ph.D. in Computational Biology from CSIR-Indian Institute of Chemical Technology under the supervision of Prof. G. Narahari Sastry. My doctoral research focused on ion–molecule interactions, lipid membrane heterogeneity, and computational approaches for drug repurposing in infectious diseases. Prior to this, I earned an M.Sc. in Bioinformatics (Gold Medalist) from Central University of Bihar and a B.Sc. in Biotechnology from Gaya College Gaya, Magadh University. I have also contributed to the development of computational platforms and databases supporting drug discovery and non-covalent interaction analysis at CSIR-NEIST.

Research expertise

  • Computational structural biology & biomolecular dynamics – all-atom and coarse-grained simulations of proteins, peptides, and membranes under environmental stresses (pressure, solvent, processing) to link conformational dynamics with function, stability, and allergenicity.
  • Protein & peptide structure–function prediction – multi-scale modeling of protein–protein, protein–ligand, and protein–membrane interactions, including QM/MM and enhanced-sampling approaches for binding and selectivity.
  • AI-driven bioinformatics & molecular design – development of predictive models using protein language models, graph neural networks, and classical ML for cell-penetrating, antimicrobial, and bioactive peptides.
  • Computational drug discovery & cheminformatics – virtual screening, drug repurposing, and fragment-based design; exploration of chemical space using MPDS, RDKit, and related tools for small-molecule optimization.
  • Membrane biophysics & biologics design – modeling lipid heterogeneity, peptide–bilayer interactions, and pore formation to guide the design of targeted biologics and membrane-active agents.
  • Software, databases & open platforms – co-development of web portals and databases (MPDS, A2ID, CAD) for non-covalent interactions and structure-based drug discovery, supporting academic and industrial users.

Research highlights

  • pLM4CPPs & pLM4G-CPPs. Developed protein language model–based CNN and graph models for cell-penetrating peptide prediction, improving over existing CPP predictors and enabling interpretable sequence–feature analyses.
  • Membrane selectivity of antimicrobial peptides. Used AA/CG MD to uncover how Snakin-Z and melittin discriminate between prokaryotic and eukaryotic membranes, integrating contact analysis, pore formation, and pressure-dependent effects.
  • Gliadin and protein dynamics in complex environments. Performed long-timescale simulations of α-gliadin and ovalbumin under ethanol, high pressure, and ultrasound, linking structural changes to functionality and potential allergenicity.
  • MPDS & non-covalent interaction databases. Contributed to the Molecular Property Diagnostic Suite, A2ID, CAD, and fragment libraries for systematic exploration of chemical space and non-covalent interactions.
