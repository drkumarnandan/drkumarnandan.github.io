Nandan Kumar

Postdoctoral Research Associate — Kansas State University

I am a computational structural biologist driven by the belief that understanding proteins at the atomic level can transform medicine, food science, and biotechnology.

Over the past decade, I’ve worked at the intersection of artificial intelligence, molecular dynamics, and experimental biochemistry — using tools like protein language models, graph neural networks, and multiscale simulations to design bioactive peptides, study membrane interactions, and unravel the structural secrets of food proteins.

My research spans personalized therapeutics, food protein functionality, and non-covalent interactions in complex systems. I’m particularly passionate about building open tools and frameworks that make cutting-edge computational biology accessible to the broader scientific community.

Recipient of the 2025 Outstanding Postdoctoral Research Award at Kansas State University.
