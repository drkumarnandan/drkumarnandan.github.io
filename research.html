Research Expertise

Integrating artificial intelligence, multiscale molecular simulations, and structural bioinformatics to advance peptide therapeutics, membrane biophysics, food protein science, and open-source drug discovery tools.

AI-Driven Peptide & Protein Design

Developing protein language models, graph neural networks, and hybrid AI frameworks to predict bioactivity and rationally design cell-penetrating, antimicrobial, and functional food peptides with enhanced selectivity and therapeutic potential.

Membrane Biophysics & Selectivity Mechanisms

Using all-atom and coarse-grained molecular dynamics to study peptide–membrane interactions, lipid bilayer heterogeneity, and mechanisms of selective membrane disruption — critical for next-generation antimicrobials and drug delivery systems.

Food Protein Structure–Function & Allergenicity

Investigating conformational dynamics, aggregation behavior, and functional changes in wheat gluten, chickpea, and egg proteins under processing conditions (solvent, pressure, ultrasound) to improve nutrition, reduce allergenicity, and enhance industrial applications.

Non-Covalent Interactions & Biomolecular Recognition

Characterizing cation-π, π–π stacking, salt bridges, and hydrophobic effects in protein folding, ligand binding, and membrane insertion through quantum chemistry, informatics databases (CAD, A2ID), and simulation-based approaches.

Open-Source Bioinformatics & Drug Discovery Platforms

Building scalable, open-access tools and databases (e.g., MPDS, CAD, A2ID) using cheminformatics, machine learning, and web technologies to accelerate virtual screening, chemical space exploration, and global collaborative research.
